Core Team

(More To Come)

Mark Goldberg

Mark is a former certified financial fraud examiner and criminal intelligence specialist with extensive public regulatory experience.

Since legalization in Oregon, Mark has focused on ways to adopt his experience with regulations, finances and data driven analysis to the cannabis industry.

Having worked in numerous positions throughout the industry over the past 4 years (manufacturing, operations, packaging, distribution, accounting, IT and compliance) Mark has developed a knowledge base to marry to his previous law enforcement and regulatory experiences that allows for a greater strategic approach, remedying issues through out the work flow rather than applying band aids and moving on to the next problem.

Mark grew up in the Washington DC area where upon graduating from George Mason University with a degree in Geographic information Systems and began his career as a federal contractor and later a civilian employee. He spent over 11 years working within Department of Defense, Department of Justice and the Office of Inspector General for Housing and Urban Development.

Michelle Elder, C.P.A.

Michelle has been active in the field of accounting since 1987 and is licensed with the State Board of Accountancy.

Her interests focus primarily on audit and accounting issues for a wide spectrum of clientele. She has extensive experience in the area of audits of non-profit organizations and local governments, in particular with OMB Circular A-133.

In addition to Michelle's audit activities, she consults with emerging companies on accounting and tax issues. Michelle also has extensive working knowledge of the implementation and support of a variety of accounting systems.

Michelle's activities in the professional community include Treasurer and Charter member of both Luna Vista Rotary and Tip It of Sacramento, She was also the 2008-09 State President of the Society of California Accountants and she is still an active board member. She is also a member of the California CPA Society and serves on the Internal Revenue Service's Practitioner Liaison Committee in Sacramento.

Dale Schafer

Dale has been an attorney for over thirty years and a specialist in cannabis law for over two decades.

Prior to legalization, Dale represented thousands of growers and medical cannabis patients. Since 2018, he has represented businesses in all parts of the supply chain as they moved from temporary to full state licenses. He is currently building businesses from scratch as well as M & A, due diligence, compliance, mediation and expert opinions in the California regulated cannabis industry.
